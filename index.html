About me

I am a PhD candidate in economics at the University of Kent, supervised Dr Guy Tchuente and Dr Sylvain Barde
I am a member of the Microeconomics Research Group and Macroeconomics, Growth and History Centre (MaGHiC)

Research interests

My primary research interests are in the areas of theoretical and applied econometrics.
My current research focuses on how to estimate unbiased/efficient causal parameters in the presence of cross-sectional dependence in the high-dimensional setting using machine learning techniques.
My secondary research interests are in the areas of regional economics, economic geography and labour economics.
